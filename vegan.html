Benefits of Being Vegan

What difference can you make by going vegan?

A Cleaner Greener Planet

What we purchase and what we eat is the predominant way that citizens contribute to the world around us. It is in our daily transactions that we decide how the world will form, currently and for future generations. Raising animals for food is an extremely inefficient use of the world’s energy, water and land mass. Eliminating or reduing meat consumption is the simplest way to personally decrease your carbon footprint and work towards avoiding catastrophic climate change. Animal Agriculture is a massive contributor of methane, nitrous oxide and carbon dioxide together this industry produces 18% of the world’s greenhouse gasses.

Raising livestock takes up 30% of the earth’s land mass

That number does not consider the amount of land that is used to specifically feed animals. 70% of grain grown in North America is used to feed farmed animals, taking 16 pounds of grain to produce 1 pound of meat. Carnivorous diets create 7x the greenhouse gas emissions than that of those who eat Vegan and consume 5x the amount of water annually. A vegan diet uses a third of the land used needed to support a meat and dairy diet. With the population, food and water insecurity increasing individuals need to progress to living sustainably. A plant-based lifestyle is a huge leap to living sustainably

If you consider yourself an environmentalist, consider eating vegan to easily contribute to a healthier planet and a more promising future.

Compassion for Animals

Veganism is about giving the voiceless their lives back by abstaining from the exploitation of animals in all industries.

n the last century human beings have made massive evolutionary progress in liberating those who have been oppressed due to discrimination of their sex, race and other prejudices. Through empathy towards others, individuals recognized that nobody’s life is more valuable than another and it is time this extends to the animals.

700 million farmed animals are killed for food each year in Canada alone

The cruelty we have inflicted upon other species far outweighs the crimes we’ve imposed upon each other. It is time we think of other animals with respect, not as our property but as sentient beings.

All lives matter.

A Healthier, Happier You

Eliminating meat and dairy from your diet and switching to a whole-foods plant-based diet could be to key to generative disease prevention.

Predominant Doctors such as Dr. Campbell and Dr. Esseltyn of Forks over Knives and Dr. Gregor of Nutrition Facts as well as many others have made waves in the world we think about health and our diet. They reveal through years of practical research the degradation animal products cause in the human body.
